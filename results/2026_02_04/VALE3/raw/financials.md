Financial Data Log for VALE3:

**3Q25 vs 3Q24 (YoY)**

1.  **Top Line:**
    *   Net Revenue: Not explicitly stated, but Vale ADR (NYSE: VALE) reported revenue of $10.42B, a 9.08% increase YoY.
    *   Iron ore sales reached 86.0 Mt, up 5%.
2.  **Operational:**
    *   EBITDA (Adjusted): US$ 4.4 billion, up 17% YoY. The increase was driven by higher volumes, cost efficiencies, and higher iron ore, copper, and by-products prices.
    *   EBITDA Margin: Not explicitly stated.
3.  **Bottom Line:**
    *   Net Income: Not explicitly stated in USD.
    *   Net Margin: Not explicitly stated.
4.  **Cash Flow:**
    *   Operating Cash Flow: Not explicitly stated.
    *   Free Cash Flow: Recurring Free Cash Flow generation was US$ 1.6 billion, US$ 1.0 billion higher YoY, primarily reflecting stronger EBITDA.
5.  **Guidance:**
    *   Capital Expenditures: US$ 1.3 billion, US$ 0.1 billion lower YoY, on track to meet the guidance of US$ 5.4-5.7 billion set for 2025.
    *   Iron ore fines' C1 cash cost, excluding third-party purchases, remained largely flat YoY and on track to meet the 2025 guidance, reaching US$ 20.7/t in the quarter.
    *   Copper all-in cost guidance for 2025 further revised down to US$ 1,000-1,500/t (from US$ 1,500-2,000/t), driven by higher gold prices.
    *   Nickel all-in cost guidance for 2025 revised down to US$ 13,000-14,000/t (from US$ 14,000-15,500/t), driven by solid operational performance and strong metals price, benefitting polymetallic assets.

**Additional Information:**

*   **3Q24:** Net income was BRL 13,386 million compared to BRL 13,864 million a year ago. Consolidated net operating revenue reached 52.9 billion reais, reflecting a modest increase compared to 2023.
*   All-in costs for iron ore declined 4% y/y to US$ 52.9/t, driven by enhanced quality premiums and reduced freight costs.
*   Iron ore shipments increased by 1.3 Mt (+2%) y/y, driven by an 18% rise in pellet sales due to higher production and strong demand.
*   Free cash flow was US$ 179 million, US$ 947 million lower y/y, largely reflecting the decrease in EBITDA.
