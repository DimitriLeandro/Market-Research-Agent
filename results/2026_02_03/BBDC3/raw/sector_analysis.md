*   **Interest Rates:** The Selic rate, Brazil's monetary policy interest rate, was at 15% as of October 9, 2025, one of the highest among major economies. The Central Bank of Brazil (BCB) raised the Selic rate to 14.75% by May 7, 2025, the highest level since 2006. As of February 4, 2026, the Selic rate remains unchanged at 15.00%.
*   **Interest Rates Impact:** Changes in the Selic rate directly affect borrowing costs for businesses and consumers, impacting investment decisions and consumption patterns. Higher Selic rates can curb inflationary pressures, while lower rates may stimulate inflationary trends. An increase in the basic interest rate increases the cost of obtaining third-party capital, which tends to cause companies to retain financial resources instead of investing them.
*   **Inflation:** The twelve-month inflation rate reached 5.1% in August 2025, above the 3% target. The BCB works to ensure that the twelve-month IPCA inflation is centered at the inflation target set by the National Monetary Council (CMN). Currency movements since April 2024 are projected to contribute 1.1 percentage points to headline inflation by end-2025.

The government partially reversed course, revoking the increase related to the use of investment funds abroad and maintaining the previous rate for companies transferring funds overseas for investment purposes.
