Online retail penetration is accelerating as consumers increasingly prefer digital shopping channels. This trend is expected to continue, pushing the hiring of warehouses and distributing the logistics network to cities with greater purchasing power.
*   **Manufacturing Sector Growth:** Growth in the manufacturing sector also drives the need for logistics services.
*   **Increased Trade Activity:** Both domestic and international trade are core drivers. Manufacturers, agricultural producers, retailers, and exporters depend on reliable freight networks to move goods efficiently. Brazil's role as a regional and global trade hub is evolving, supported by rising domestic consumption and expanding exports.
*   **Infrastructure Investments:** Government infrastructure investments are a key factor driving market growth. Investments in road connectivity, port capacity, and inland waterways are helping reduce bottlenecks and improve transit reliability. Key initiatives include road and highway upgrades and rail expansion projects.
