### Bear Case: Hybrid/Paper Sector in Brazil

**1. Structural Risks:**

*   **Decline in Printing and Writing Paper:** The production of printing and writing papers has been declining, with a decrease of -1.9% per year between 2015 and 2023. This trend was accelerated by the pandemic due to remote learning and reduced office attendance. In 2023, production volume continued to decline by -3.4% compared to 2022. This secular decline threatens the long-term viability of companies focused on these paper grades.
*   **Weak Correlation with GDP:** Unlike other paper grades like containerboard, the consumption of printing and writing paper is not strongly correlated with GDP in Brazil. This means that during economic downturns, the demand for these papers falls sharply because they are considered non-essential.
*   **Shifting Demand:** Trends indicate rising demand for household and sanitary paper (increased by 27% since 2010) and packaging materials (increased by 23% since 2010), while demand for printing and writing papers decreases (decreased by 16% since 2010). Companies not adapting to this shift face declining revenues.
